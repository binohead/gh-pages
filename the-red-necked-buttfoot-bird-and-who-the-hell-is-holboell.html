The red-necked buttfoot bird. And who the hell is Holboell?

Andy
February 20, 2018
Updated on February 20, 2018

The Red-necked Grebe in the photo was unusually close to shore in the harbour. Five off my patch this afternoon reminded me: my first field guides called them Holboell's Grebes.

That's Carl Peter Holbøl, who was Denmark's Royal Inspector of Colonies and Whaling back in the day (1820s). This species was one of the specimens he sent back from Greenland, and a professor named it after him. Holboell named species; Hornemann's Redpoll is a botanist he knew.

Alas, Hoelboell's bird (like Hornemann's) was deemed, years later, to be a mere subspecies of Red-necked Grebe. And so the young kids don't even know Holboell's name any more. Then again, its genus is still Podiceps, which means "anus foot", because grebes' feet are so close to their butts. So not all is lost.
